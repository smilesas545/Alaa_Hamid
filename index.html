The Science of Coffee

Extraction Chemistry

The process where water dissolves flavors from coffee grounds. Optimal extraction (18-22%) yields balanced flavors, while under or over-extraction creates sour or bitter tastes.

Key Compounds: Caffeine, chlorogenic acids, lipids, melanoidins

Temperature Control

Water temperature dramatically affects extraction. Too hot (>205°F) can burn coffee, too cold (<195°F) under-extracts. The ideal range is 195-205°F (90-96°C).

Sweet Spot: 200°F (93°C) for most brewing methods

Brew Ratios

The coffee-to-water ratio determines strength. Common ratios range from 1:15 to 1:18 (coffee:water). A 1:16 ratio means 1g coffee to 16g water.

Golden Ratio: 1:16 for balanced flavor and strength
